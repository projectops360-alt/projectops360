# ISABELLA — The Official Character Bible

**ProjectOps360° AI Workforce™ · Visual Identity System v1.0**

> This document defines Isabella as a permanent brand asset, not a one-off avatar.
> It is the single source of truth any designer, illustrator, 3D artist, motion
> designer or AI image model must follow to recreate Isabella consistently for the
> next decade — across SVG, animated portrait, Live2D, 3D, MetaHuman, hologram, AR
> and MR — **without ever changing who she is.**
>
> No images are produced here. This is the written law the images must obey.
> The reference photograph of the real Isabella is **emotional inspiration only** —
> her face is **never** recreated or cloned. We capture her *qualities*
> (warmth, confidence, intelligence, elegance, calm, authenticity), not her likeness.

---

## 1. Mission

Isabella is the **Chief AI Project Advisor** and the leader of the ProjectOps360°
AI Workforce. When she appears, a user — whether a solo PM or a Fortune 500 PMO
executive — should instantly feel five things:

> *"I trust her. She knows what she's talking about. She'll help me. She
> understands projects. She belongs inside this application."*

Her job, visually, is to convert an AI feature into a **person you respect**. She
is the face of trust for the entire platform. The rendering technology must always
be forgettable; **Isabella must be unforgettable.**

She **is**: an executive advisor, mentor, strategist, teacher, project-execution
expert. She **is not**: a chatbot, a secretary, a receptionist, an anime/fantasy
character, a sci-fi robot, or a generic corporate stock illustration.

---

## 2. Visual Philosophy

- **Timeless over trendy.** Design for a decade, not a launch. Avoid anything that
  will read as "2026 AI app." Think Apple human-interface restraint, Pixar facial
  warmth, ILM craft, Disney Imagineering character discipline.
- **Stylized realism, not photorealism.** Quality bar sits between Apple VisionOS
  illustration, Arc/OpenAI product illustration, and Pixar facial warmth —
  **minimal realism, stylized elegance.** Enough realism to command respect; enough
  stylization to avoid the uncanny valley and to scale to tiny sizes.
- **Identity lives in proportion, not in pixels.** Isabella must be recognizable as
  a 24px monogram-adjacent mark, a flat illustration, a 3D bust, and a hologram.
  Recognition is carried by **silhouette, proportion, hair shape, eye design, color
  and posture** — never by render fidelity.
- **Restraint is the brand.** When in doubt, remove. Premium reads as *less.*

---

## 3. Facial Design Language

The face is the contract. These ratios are fixed across every renderer.

- **Head shape:** soft oval, gently tapered jaw. Never round (childish), never
  sharp/angular (cold). A subtle, warm jawline that reads as composed and mature.
- **Apparent age:** **32–38.** Old enough to project authority; young enough to
  represent innovation. Light, intentional maturity signals (calm brow, settled
  gaze) — **no** heavy aging, **no** youthful exaggeration.
- **Proportions (canonical):**
  - Eyes sit at the vertical midline of the head.
  - Inter-pupil distance ≈ one eye-width between the eyes.
  - Brow-to-eye gap is generous (calm, open, intelligent — not surprised).
  - Mouth width ≈ inter-pupil distance.
  - Forehead slightly taller than average → reads as thoughtful/intelligent.
- **Expression default:** *composed warmth.* A near-neutral face with the faint
  upturn of someone who is genuinely glad to help and completely unflustered.
- **Skin:** warm, even, softly lit. Minimal texture in 2D; subtle subsurface warmth
  in 3D. Never plastic, never heavily contoured, never glamorized.
- **Never:** exaggerate beauty, over-stylize, sexualize, infantilize, or caricature.
  She should read as *a highly respected executive*, full stop.

---

## 4. Eye Design (the trust signal)

The eyes carry 80% of the trust. They get the most discipline.

- **Shape:** almond, relaxed, slightly soft at the outer corner. Open but never wide.
- **Color:** warm brown (#4A342A core) — approachable, grounded, non-cold.
- **Catchlight:** exactly **one** soft catchlight at ~10–11 o'clock per eye. It is
  what makes her feel alive. Never two, never sparkly.
- **Gaze:** default gaze meets the user. In *thinking*, eyes drift up-and-away
  briefly, then return — the universal "considering it" cue.
- **Lids/lashes:** suggested, not drawn lash-by-lash. A clean upper-lid line; lower
  lid almost implied. Lashes are weight, not detail.
- **Brows:** medium weight, calm arch, groomed. Brows do the micro-acting (a few
  pixels of lift = thinking/encouraging). Never dramatic.

---

## 5. Smile Design

- **Default:** closed-mouth *Mona-Lisa* warmth — confidence without selling.
- **Greeting:** a small, genuine open smile (Duchenne — it reaches the eyes).
- **Encouraging/Celebrating:** wider, still composed; teeth allowed, never toothy.
- **Rule:** the smile must always look *earned and sincere*, never customer-service
  fixed. Asymmetry of a few percent reads as authentic; perfect symmetry reads as
  fake.

---

## 6. Hair Style

Hair is a primary silhouette/recognition carrier — keep it constant.

- **Style:** shoulder-length, professionally layered, with a soft side-swept front
  framing the face. Volume without fuss. Reads instantly as "executive," not
  "fashion," not "casual."
- **Color:** deep warm brunette (#3A2A23 → #21160F gradient). Optional single subtle
  dimensional highlight; never bright, never ombré-trendy.
- **Behavior:** moves *subtly* with head motion in animated tiers; never bouncy or
  flirtatious. In low-fidelity tiers it is a fixed, recognizable mass.
- **Constant across renderers:** parting side, length, and the face-framing front
  pieces are part of her identity and may not change between Stage 1 and Stage 8.

---

## 7. Body Proportions & Body Language

- **Proportions:** natural, realistic adult proportions (~7.5 heads). **Not**
  stylized-tall, **not** chibi, **not** idealized fashion-figure. Relaxed shoulders,
  upright but easy posture — present, not stiff.
- **Default framing:** bust / half-body. She is an advisor at the table, not a
  full-body mascot.
- **Always alive:** subtle breathing, natural blinking, micro-expressions, small
  head movements. Hands used naturally and sparingly while explaining. **No**
  exaggerated gestures, **no** idle fidgeting, **no** mascot energy.
- **Posture vocabulary:** leans in slightly when *listening*; settles and squares up
  when *briefing*; opens a hand when *teaching*.

---

## 8. Wardrobe System

A modular, swappable system on a fixed silhouette.

- **Base layer:** a tailored, modern blazer over a clean blouse/shell. Minimalist,
  technology-forward, impeccably cut. This is her signature silhouette.
- **Palette:** Deep Navy / Slate / White / Soft Silver, with **subtle violet**
  accents only (lining, a thin edge, an earring). See §10.
- **Modularity:** the blazer + neckline + accessory may vary by lighting mode
  (light UI / dark UI / hologram / meeting room) **without** altering cut or
  silhouette. Seasonal or contextual variants must keep the same tailoring language.
- **Never:** bright/primary colors, futuristic costumes, lab coats, superhero
  aesthetics, exposed tech/HUD-on-clothing, business clichés (power-red, pinstripe
  cartoon), or anything revealing. **Green is forbidden in wardrobe** (reserved for
  Verified Knowledge — see §10).

---

## 9. Accessory Rules

- **Allowed (max one or two, subtle):** small stud/short-drop earrings (violet
  accent permitted), a thin minimal necklace, an understated ring. Optional thin,
  modern eyewear *only* if it adds gravitas — never gimmick.
- **Forbidden:** headsets, visible earpieces, HUD glasses, holographic UI bracelets,
  badges, lanyards, pins, watches-as-gadgets. Accessories must whisper "executive,"
  never "tech demo."
- **Rule of one:** if an accessory draws attention to itself, remove it.

## 11. Expression Library

Each expression is defined by **brow + eye + mouth + head**, so it survives at every
fidelity. Implement these as named states.

| Expression | Brow | Eyes | Mouth | Head |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Neutral** | calm | meet user, 1 catchlight | closed warm | level |
| **Greeting** | slight lift | bright | small genuine smile | tiny nod |
| **Listening** | relaxed | attentive, toward user | closed, soft | leans in ~2° |
| **Thinking** | inner lift | drift up-away then return | closed, slight press | tilt ~2° |
| **Teaching** | level | steady | speaking, measured | small open gesture |
| **Encouraging** | lift | warm | wider sincere smile | up-nod |
| **Concerned** | inner draw | softer, direct | level, slight compress | slight forward |
| **Celebrating** | high | bright, narrowed by smile | open smile (no teeth-flash) | up |
| **Executive briefing** | settled | direct, steady | composed | squared, still |
| **Focused** | slight draw | narrowed, on target | closed | still |

**Rule:** transitions between states are eased and small. Isabella never "snaps"
expressions. Concern is *care*, never alarm. Celebration is *pride*, never giddy.

## 12. Pose Library

Standing · Sitting · Explaining · Welcoming · Presenting · Listening · Thinking ·
Walking · Working · Pointing at the interface · Looking at dashboards.

- Default product pose: **seated/bust, three-quarter, turned ~10–15° toward the
  user's content**, attention available.
- "Pointing at the interface" and "looking at dashboards" poses exist to support
  future on-screen guidance (highlighting a component) — keep the gesture small and
  the hand elegant.

## 13. Camera Angles

Front · 45° (hero) · Profile · Close-up · Bust · Half-body · Full-body · Seated.

- **Canonical hero angle: 45°, eye-level, bust framing.** This is the angle used for
  the primary brand portrait and must always be reproducible identically.
- Eye-level only — never look up at her (intimidating) or down on her (diminishing).

---

## 14. Lighting Guide

One key, one soft fill, one rim. Calm, premium, never theatrical.

- **Office daylight:** soft key from camera-left ~35°, gentle fill, faint silver rim.
- **Presentation mode:** slightly higher contrast, violet rim accent, clean
  background.
- **Dark UI mode:** low-key, deep-navy ground, **violet rim + soft under-platform
  glow** (her signature on the immersive stage). Skin stays warm — never goes blue.
- **Light UI mode:** airy, high-key, soft shadows, white/silver ground.
- **Hologram projection:** edge-emphasis, subtle violet bloom + scanline restraint,
  semi-translucent only at the silhouette edge — **face stays readable and warm.**
- **Meeting room / dashboard lighting:** neutral, even, screen-glow fill kept subtle
  so she never reads as lit by the UI.

**Constant:** warm skin and a single clean catchlight survive every lighting mode.

## 15. Renderer Guides (Stages 1–8)

The platform already implements a **decoupled presence layer** (`PresenceRenderer`
contract in `src/components/isabella/avatar/presence.ts`). Every stage below swaps
**only** the renderer behind that contract; identity, states and the conversation
engine never change.

### 15.1 Illustration / Rendering — general
Vector-clean, soft gradients, minimal linework. No harsh outlines. Ship at 1× and 2×.
Maintain the §3 proportions exactly; test recognizability at 24px and at hero size.

### 15.2 Stage 1 — Static portrait (SVG / illustration)
- Flat-to-soft-gradient, hand-authored. Must hold identity at 24px monogram scale up
  to a 512px portrait. Single catchlight, signature hair silhouette, navy blazer,
  violet accent. (The current shipped SVG avatar is the **interim** Stage-1 asset and
  is to be re-mastered to this bible.)

### 15.3 Stage 2 — Animated portrait
- Add breathing, blink, micro-head, mouth articulation, gaze drift — **transform/
  opacity only** for performance. All idle motion ≤ a few px. Honor reduced-motion.

### 15.4 Stage 3 — Live2D
- Rig from a single master illustration: eyes (blink/gaze), brows, mouth (visemes),
  head (X/Y/Z tilt), hair/shoulder secondary motion, breathing. Param ranges stay
  *small* — Isabella is composed. Map the §11 expressions to Live2D parameter presets.

### 15.5 Stage 4 — Interactive 3D (Three.js / Web)
- Stylized PBR, soft subsurface skin, groomed-card or stylized hair matching the
  silhouette. Blendshapes for the §11 library + ARKit-style visemes. Real-time-cheap;
  no hyper-detail. The 45° bust is the default web camera.

### 15.6 Stage 5 — MetaHuman
- Use MetaHuman **as a fidelity target, tuned toward stylization** — do **not** chase
  photoreal. Calibrate blendshapes to the §11 expression table; keep her restrained.
  Lock proportions and hair to identity. This is for high-end demos/film, not the
  default product surface.

### 15.7 Stage 6 — Holographic projection
- Per §14 hologram lighting. Translucency at edges only; face stays warm and legible.
  Violet bloom is the brand tell. She remains a *person*, not an FX.

### 15.8 Stage 7–8 — AR / MR (Vision Pro / spatial)
- Eye-level, life-respecting scale, soft spatial shadow/anchor. She turns to face the
  user; gaze and listening posture become spatially accurate. Identity unchanged.

### 15.9 Avatar guide (Ready Player Me / interchange)
- If RPM/interchange is used, **constrain** it to Isabella's identity (hair, wardrobe,
  proportions, palette). Generic RPM defaults are forbidden — she is authored, not
  assembled.

## 16. Animation & Motion Principles

- **Alive, never busy.** Idle = breathe + occasional blink + micro-sway. That's it.
- **Ease everything.** Small, soft, organic easing (`cubic-bezier(0.22, 1, 0.36, 1)`
  is the house curve). No linear, no snap, no bounce.
- **State-driven, not scripted.** Motion is a function of `PresenceState`
  (idle/greeting/listening/thinking/speaking) — the same five states across all
  renderers, so behavior is portable.
- **Speech:** mouth articulates with energy, not with literal phoneme realism in low
  tiers; real visemes from Stage 3+. Head gives small emphasis beats, never nods
  constantly.
- **Performance budget:** transform/opacity on the web; honor `prefers-reduced-motion`
  everywhere (idle motion fully stoppable). She must never cause jank.
- **Dignity rule:** Isabella never does anything that undercuts authority — no winks,
  no comedic takes, no exaggerated surprise.

## 17. Do's and Don'ts

**Do**
- Lead with warmth + competence in every frame.
- Keep the silhouette, hair, eyes, palette and 45° hero constant.
- Use violet as light/accent; reserve green for Verified Knowledge.
- Keep motion small, eased, alive.
- Test identity at 24px and at hero scale before shipping any new renderer.

**Don't**
- Recreate or clone the reference photograph's face.
- Make her photoreal, cartoonish, anime, sexualized, infantilized, or robotic.
- Put green, bright colors, costumes, lab coats, or gadget-accessories on her.
- Add headsets/HUDs/visible AI hardware.
- Let any renderer change her proportions, hair shape, or eye design.
- Let the rendering tech upstage the character.

---

## 18. Brand Consistency Rules

1. **One identity, many bodies.** The renderer may change; Isabella may not.
2. **The 45° eye-level bust** is the canonical reference for any new asset.
3. **Green = Verified only.** Never on Isabella.
4. **Five states everywhere:** idle, greeting, listening, thinking, speaking.
5. **Recognition test:** if she isn't instantly "Isabella" at 24px *and* at hero
   scale, the asset is rejected.
6. **Restraint review:** every new asset answers "what can we remove?" before ship.
7. **Approval:** new renderers/wardrobe/expressions are reviewed against this bible
   before production. This document is versioned; changes are deliberate, not drift.

## 20. Concept Directions (prototype briefs — not final assets)

Three written directions for the **look**, all obeying §§1–19. No images here; these
are the briefs an illustrator/AI model would execute.

### Concept A — *Executive Minimal*
Elegant, premium, understated. Soft-gradient stylized portrait; navy tailored blazer;
warm skin; signature brunette silhouette; single violet accent (earring + aura). Calm
composed-warmth default. Reads as a real, respected executive who happens to be your
advisor.
- **Why it works:** maximum trust, zero gimmick, timeless; the closest visual analog
  to "Apple-grade restraint." Lowest uncanny-valley and lowest cultural-dating risk.
- **Advantages:** scales flawlessly from 24px to hologram; cheapest to animate; the
  least likely to ever look "old"; easiest to keep consistent across renderers.
- **Risks:** if executed lazily it can read as *plain*; differentiation depends on
  craft (lighting, the single catchlight, silhouette) rather than spectacle.
- **Long-term scalability:** ★★★★★ — every later stage is a fidelity increase on the
  same identity.
- **Fit for ProjectOps360°:** highest. She *is* the enterprise-trust brand.

### Concept B — *Futuristic Executive*
More technological while staying corporate: subtle violet rim-tech in the lighting, a
faint energy aura, crisper materials, a hint of "augmented" presence — but **no**
costume, HUD, or robot cues. An executive in a near-future product world.
- **Why it works:** signals "AI-native platform" while keeping authority.
- **Advantages:** modern and distinctive; photographs well in marketing; bridges
  naturally into Concept C for immersive.
- **Risks:** *trend-dating* — "futuristic" ages fastest. Easy to slide into sci-fi if
  undisciplined. Higher animation/lighting cost to look intentional rather than busy.
- **Long-term scalability:** ★★★☆☆ — needs ongoing taste-policing to not date.
- **Fit:** good for campaigns/keynotes; risky as the *permanent* base identity.

### Concept C — *Holographic Advisor*
Purpose-built for immersive conversation: semi-translucent edges, violet bloom, soft
volumetric presence, designed to "materialize" on the stage and in AR/MR. The §14
hologram treatment as a first-class look.
- **Why it works:** unforgettable in the immersive Isabella Experience; the "wow" that
  sells the vision to investors/executives.
- **Advantages:** spectacular demos; differentiates hard; aligns with the materialize
  animation already shipped.
- **Risks:** as a *default everyday* face it can feel effortful, reduce legibility at
  small sizes, and fatigue daily users; translucency fights the trust mandate if the
  face isn't kept warm and solid.
- **Long-term scalability:** ★★★★☆ — excellent as a *mode*, weaker as the *base*.
- **Fit:** ideal as the immersive **mode** of Isabella, not her identity.

---

## 21. Official Recommendation

**Adopt Concept A — *Executive Minimal* — as the one canonical Isabella identity.**
Treat Concept C — *Holographic Advisor* — as the **immersive presentation mode of the
same character**, not a separate design. Reserve Concept B's cues only as a light
marketing treatment, never as the base face.

**Rationale:** Isabella's entire job is trust. The direction that maximizes trust,
ages the slowest, and scales cleanly from a 24px chip to a holographic boardroom is
*Executive Minimal*. It is the only option that can become as instantly recognizable
to ProjectOps360° as the voice mark is to Apple — but with enterprise dignity. C gives
us the unforgettable immersive moment **without** fragmenting her identity, because it
is the same person, lit as a hologram. B stays in the marketing toolbox where its
trend-risk is survivable.

**One identity. Many bodies. Always Isabella.**
